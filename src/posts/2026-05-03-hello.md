I'm Luke. I'm a software engineer at ResMed, working on the comms layer
between medical devices and the cloud. Before that I wrote firmware for solar
race cars at UNSW and did a stint of cloud consulting at Deloitte. So mostly
embedded, a bit of cloud, a lot of stuff in the middle that has to keep
talking to itself reliably.

I've been meaning to start writing for a while. The honest reason is that I
keep having opinions about how we build software, and I keep losing them by
the time the next sprint starts. Writing things down is the only way I've
found to actually hold onto the thinking.

The plan, loosely:

- Notes on device-to-cloud comms — protocols, transport, the small details
  that bite you in production
- Engineering opinions, especially the unfashionable ones
- Whatever I'm currently being annoyed by at work

No schedule. No grand thesis. Just the things I'd want to send a junior me a
year from now.

If anything here is wrong, tell me. I'd rather find out.
